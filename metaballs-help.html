Metaballs Help

Getting Started

  • Add primitives: Drag shapes from the tray into your scene.
  • Move, scale, rotate: Use standard visionOS 26 manipulation gestures to adjust primitives.
  • Edit materials: Tap a metaball to open its material settings.
  • Set defaults: Tap the palette icon in the tray to choose the material for new primitives.
  • Positive & negative: Use the +/- toggle to switch a metaball between additive (positive) and subtractive (negative).
  • Metaballs+: Some shapes and materials are part of the Metaballs+ subscription. Think of it as a way to support indie app development and future updates to this playful tool.

Saving your work

To save the current version of your scene, tap the gallery icon in the toolbar. This will bring up the gallery as well as save the current state of your scene. Please note that if you work on a scene and leave the app, the scene does not 'autosave' at this time. Note that we may change this behavior in future versions – but for now, we've opted to have this be a manual step. Please check back soon, as this help file will be updated with new releases of the app.

Export

Note: Metaballs v1.2 is coming soon and will replace the export options below with two formats: USDZ and STL! This will be a big improvement, as USDZ can be brought into your shared space on visionOS – and will include the materials from Metaballs: Spatial! STL was requested from users who want to 3D print. It's a simple, universal format (geometry only) – and it's quite fun to see something sculpted on Apple Vision Pro turn into a real world object.

Metaballs v1.0 and v1.1 can export geometry only in two formats:

  • Reality files (.reality): Lightweight and viewable on Apple Vision Pro / XR—perfect for placing your sculptures in your space with Quick Look.
  • USD files (.usd): Not viewable on Quick Look on Vision Pro. Designed for desktop workflows in tools like Houdini or Blender.

Materials aren’t included in either format yet. However, if you import a Metaballs USD export into Reality Composer Pro, you can access the model’s color via the geometryColor shader graph node. Check back soon for an example.
